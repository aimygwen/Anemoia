Born from vision, shaped by noise.

Identity

Softness with structure. Play with restraint.

My visual identity didn’t arrive fully formed — it grew out of wanting things to feel approachable without losing clarity. Pastel warmth, rounded forms, and a little bit of mischief sit on top of a grid I actually trust.

The bunny mark, the pixel accents, the way type shifts between expressive display and readable body copy — those aren’t random choices. They’re how I signal that this work is human-made, cared for, and allowed to be a little weird.

I think of branding less as a logo lockup and more as a mood you can recognize from across the room: gentle, precise, slightly nostalgic, never sterile.

Process

Start messy. Refine until it breathes.

Most of my projects begin as loose notes — a color swatch, a sentence, a screenshot I can’t stop thinking about. I sketch in blocks before I polish curves, because understanding shape language matters more to me than jumping straight to detail.

When something feels off, I usually step back and ask whether the problem is visual or emotional. Is it too loud? Too flat? Does it match the story I’m trying to tell? That question saves me from overworking the wrong layer.

Iteration isn’t failure — it’s how I find the version that feels inevitable, like it was always meant to look that way.

“If I can explain why a choice exists, it’s probably ready. If I can’t, I keep going.”

Workspace

A desk that invites focus — and the occasional sticker pile.

I work best in spaces that feel lived-in: monitor light, a notebook always open, reference images pinned where I’ll actually see them. Tools matter, but ritual matters more — the same playlist, the same coffee mug, the same slow start before the pixels move.

My setup is split between illustration, 3D, and web — so everything has to be within reach without becoming clutter. Cables hidden where possible. Inspiration visible where it helps.

The workspace isn’t a aesthetic set piece. It’s the quiet infrastructure behind everything you see on this site.

Notes

Experiences, fragments, things I’m still figuring out.

Games taught me worldbuilding. Music taught me pacing. Design taught me to edit — ruthlessly, lovingly. I carry all of that into every commission, every personal piece, every page like this one.

I’m drawn to collaborators who care about feeling as much as function. The best projects leave me with a new constraint I didn’t know I needed, and a story worth telling afterward.

This page will keep growing — less a portfolio of finished work, more a diary of how the work gets made.
